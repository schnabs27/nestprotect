What changed and why

The current auth page leads with a cartoon (“Nestor wasn’t prepared”). That works as a mascot but does little to build trust for someone landing on the app for the first time. The redesign moves the trust signals up — a real photo, a real story, and the credibility behind the data — before the user is asked to sign in.

Above-the-fold hero

  • Real photo of Leo’s home after the 2019 Dallas tornado. Documentary, not stylized. Carries far more weight than the cartoon.
  • Headline reframes the problem in the words of the one-pager: “No one thinks the storm will hit them.”
  • Sub-line establishes the BEFORE / DURING / AFTER promise in one sentence, anchored to the user’s ZIP.

ZIP code lookup stays front-and-center

The risk-check is the strongest functional hook on the original page — it lets users get value before signing up. Moved up and styled with the special (royal-to-purple) gradient to mark it as the “moment of magic” CTA.

Leo’s story as a quote card

Short, plain-spoken, and tied to the photo above. Establishes founder credibility without feeling like a sales pitch.

Four trust pills

Free / Private / 17 disaster types / 501(c)(3). These are the four things a skeptical first-time visitor needs to know in 5 seconds. They’re short on purpose.

Data sources card

The one-pager mentions FEMA, Red Cross, HUD, Google Business, and AI search. Surfacing these as named tags signals “this isn’t a random app — it’s aggregating the same sources the pros use.”

Before / During / After kept

The three-row structure from the original auth page is preserved — it’s tight, scannable, and on-brand with the one-pager. Reformatted into a cleaner card to match the rest of the design system.

Sign-in and walk-through

Both kept from the original. Tabbed Sign In / Sign Up, email + password, “Continue with Google,” “Having trouble?” help link, and the “View Details and Screenshots” walk-through button.

Brand application (per npbrand)

  • Hero: Royal blue solid — trust, not the full gradient (the cool gradient is reserved for primary CTAs and the footer).
  • Primary CTA (Sign In): Cool gradient (royal → teal).
  • Special CTA (What’s my risk?): Special gradient (royal → purple) — used exactly once, per brand rule.
  • Cards: White, 1px border-gray stroke, soft shadow, 0.75rem radius — the standard floating treatment.
  • No icons on page-level content, per the web-UI rule. Section labels (BEFORE / DURING / AFTER) carry the structure instead.
  • Footer strip: Cool gradient for the brand-anchor moment at the bottom.
